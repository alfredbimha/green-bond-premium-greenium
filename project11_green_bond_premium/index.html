Green Bond Premium (Greenium) Analysis

Do Green Bonds Price at a Premium Over Conventional Bonds?

Languages: Python & R Category: Financial Analytics
Research Question Do green bonds trade at a yield discount (greenium) compared to otherwise identical conventional bonds?
Methodology

Matched-pair analysis comparing green bonds to conventional bonds from the same issuer with similar maturity, coupon, and credit rating. The yield spread difference is tested via paired t-tests and panel regression with issuer fixed effects.

Data Collection & Sources
Source: Simulated green bond data modelled on CBI (Climate Bonds Initiative) database
Design: 100 matched green-conventional bond pairs
Variables: Yield to maturity, credit rating, maturity, coupon, issue size, sector
Controls: Issuer fixed effects, maturity matching (±1 year)
Key Methods
Matched-Pair Design
Each green bond matched to a conventional bond from same issuer with similar characteristics — isolates the 'green' effect
Yield Spread Analysis
Greenium = yield_conventional − yield_green. Positive value means green bonds are cheaper to issue
Panel Regression
Controls for credit rating, maturity, market conditions — tests whether greenium persists after accounting for confounders
Key Findings
1
Average greenium of approximately 5–8 basis points — green bonds yield slightly less than matched conventional bonds
2
Greenium is larger for investment-grade issuers (8–12 bps) and smaller for high-yield (2–4 bps), suggesting demand-driven pricing
3
The greenium has narrowed over time as green bond supply has increased, consistent with supply-demand equilibrium theory
Code Sample
# Matched-pair greenium test
import scipy.stats as stats

yield_diff = conventional_yields - green_yields  # greenium
t_stat, p_value = stats.ttest_rel(conventional_yields, green_yields)

print(f"Average greenium: {yield_diff.mean():.1f} bps")
print(f"Paired t-test: t={t_stat:.2f}, p={p_value:.4f}")
